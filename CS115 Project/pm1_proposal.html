Project Milestone 1

How TikTok Has Revolutionized Journalism

Project Summary

Within the past year, TikTok has become a social media giant. With this sudden and massive boost in popularity (especially amongst Gen-Z and millenials), TikTok has become a platform where news and trending topics can be spread. With TikTok, users are given fifteen seconds to a whole minute to share content. This means the most important parts of news stories are shared so that they can adhere to the fifteen seconds to a minute time rule. Also, with the popularity of TikTok dances amongst Gen-Z, many users on the app would use dance moves to share information and news. I want to explore how TikTok differs from other social media apps that have journalistic aspects (Instagram, Snapchat, Twitter, etc.) and TikTok's overall revolutionary affect on the journalism industry.

Research Questions

    What percentage of each age group gets their news from TikTok?
    What separates TikTok from other social media news sources like Twitter, Instagram, and Snapchat?
    How does TikTok's algorithm affect the spread of news on the app?
    TikTok users can go viral and become news stories themselves (think Gorilla Glue lady, famous TikTokkers, etc.)
    How the latest newsworthy viral trends are showcased through TikTok (certain popular phrases/sayings, clothing, products)
    The blend of opinion pieces and news on TikTok. Instead of writing an opinion piece for an online publication's website, TikTok users say their opinions on given news stories/social issues.
    What are the cons of using TikTok as a news source? (clickbait, native advertising, false news, bias)
    How are online news sources (Washington Post, NBC, etc.) utilizing TikTok?
